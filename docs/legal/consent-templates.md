# Consent Form Templates

<p class="intro-text">
  All consent forms below are adapted from the <strong>UZH Declaration of Consent for Research Projects</strong> (version May 2018), supplemented with oral-history-specific provisions for each sensitivity tier. The language stays as close to the UZH original as possible, deviating only where oral history requires additional clauses.
</p>

<div class="highlight-box">
  <p><strong>Two-stage consent:</strong> Use the <strong>Information Sheet</strong> (Stage 1) before the interview. Use the <strong>Consent Form</strong> (Stage 2) after the interview, when the narrator can make informed decisions about access, pseudonymization, and excluded passages based on what was actually discussed.</p>
</div>

## Information Sheet (Stage 1 — All Tiers)

This is provided to the narrator **before** the interview. It does not require a signature — it fulfills the duty to inform under Swiss data protection law.

<div class="form-text">
  <h4>Information Sheet for Participants in an Oral History Research Project</h4>
  <p><strong>Title of research project:</strong> <span class="blank">&nbsp;</span></p>
  <p><strong>Lead researcher:</strong> <span class="blank">&nbsp;</span></p>
  <p><strong>Organizational unit at UZH:</strong> <span class="blank">&nbsp;</span></p>
  <p><strong>Funded by:</strong> <span class="blank">&nbsp;</span></p>
  <p>Dear <span class="blank">&nbsp;</span>,</p>
  <p>You are invited to participate in an oral history research project conducted at the University of Zurich. This information sheet explains the purpose, methods, and your rights. Please read it carefully. You will have the opportunity to ask questions before and after the interview.</p>
  <h4>What is the purpose of this project?</h4>
  <p><span class="blank">&nbsp;</span> <em>(describe the research objectives in plain language)</em></p>
  <h4>What will happen?</h4>
  <p>You will be interviewed by <span class="blank">&nbsp;</span> about your experiences, memories, and perspectives on <span class="blank">&nbsp;</span>. The interview will be recorded in <em>(audio / video / audio and video)</em> format. The interview will last approximately <span class="blank">&nbsp;</span> minutes. After the interview, a transcript will be produced.</p>
  <h4>What personal data will be collected?</h4>
  <p>The recording will capture your voice <em>(and image, if video)</em>. During the interview, you may share personal experiences, opinions, and memories. Some of this may constitute sensitive personal data under Swiss data protection law (e.g., health experiences, political views, religious beliefs). You are free to decline to answer any question.</p>
  <h4>How will your data be processed and stored?</h4>
  <p>Your recording and transcript will be stored on secure University of Zurich infrastructure. Access is restricted to the research team: <span class="blank">&nbsp;</span> <em>(list team members)</em>. The data will be processed exclusively for academic research purposes.</p>
  <h4>Who will have access?</h4>
  <p><em>(Tier-specific: describe the access model — public / registered researchers / research team only / named individuals)</em></p>
  <h4>How long will your data be kept?</h4>
  <p>As a rule, the data is kept for a ten-year period and then deleted or destroyed, or has identifying information removed, unless permission has been given for its continued use in other research projects.</p>
  <h4>Your rights</h4>
  <p>You may at any time:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Request information about the data stored about you</li>
    <li>Demand the correction, deletion, or sealing of your personal data</li>
    <li>Object to the processing or transmission of your data</li>
    <li>Withdraw your consent (see Consent Form for details)</li>
    <li>Lodge a complaint with the competent supervisory authority</li>
  </ul>
  <h4>Contact</h4>
  <p>For questions about this project: <span class="blank">&nbsp;</span> <em>(lead researcher email)</em><br>
  For data protection concerns: UZH Delegate for Data Protection, datenschutz@dsd.uzh.ch</p>
</div>

## Additional Templates

### UZH Confidentiality Declaration

Required for external persons (transcribers, research assistants, collaborators) who will have access to oral history data. Based on the UZH Confidentiality Declaration (Legal Services and Data Protection, January 2025).

Key obligations for signatories:

- Maintain confidentiality of all non-public information
- Comply with the Zurich IDG
- Process data only for the stipulated purposes
- Return or destroy all data upon termination
- Report any data loss or unauthorized access immediately

→ Obtain the template from UZH Legal Services / Delegate for Data Protection (datenschutz@dsd.uzh.ch)

### Data Sharing Agreement

For sharing oral history data with external institutions, use the **UZH Master Data Sharing Agreement** (Controller–Controller). This covers data categories, purposes, security measures, breach notification, and data subject rights.

→ Obtain the template from UZH Legal Services
