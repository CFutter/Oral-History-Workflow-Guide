## Video Recording Equipment

Like audio hardware, cameras and accessories are local equipment — recordings stay on the SD card or device. Suitable for all tiers. The tier concern with video is primarily about **archiving and access**, not the recording itself.

| Equipment | Type | Approx. price | Tiers | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Sony ZV-E10 II** | Mirrorless camera (APS-C) | CHF 900–1,000 | 1–5 | Excellent autofocus, flip screen, mic input, clean HDMI out. Good for talking-head setups. |
| **Sony ZV-1 II** | Compact vlog camera | CHF 700–800 | 1–5 | All-in-one solution, good built-in mic, but external audio recommended for archival quality. |
| **Canon EOS R50** | Mirrorless camera | CHF 750–850 | 1–5 | Lightweight, good autofocus, 4K video. Popular entry-level option. |
| **Logitech Brio 500/505** | Webcam (USB) | CHF 120–150 | 1–3 | Budget option for remote or casual recordings. Good 1080p, light correction. Not archival quality — avoid for Tiers 4–5 where preservation matters. |
| **Manfrotto Compact Action** | Tripod | CHF 60–80 | 1–5 | Budget-friendly, stable for talking-head video. |
| **Elgato Key Light Mini** | LED panel light | CHF 80–100 | 1–5 | Soft, adjustable lighting. USB-C powered. Essential for consistent video quality. |
| **Neewer 2-pack LED panels** | Budget LED lights | CHF 60–80 | 1–5 | Two-light setup for interviews. Adjustable color temperature. |

**Recommended minimum setup:** Sony ZV-E10 II or equivalent + tripod + one LED light + external audio recorder or lavalier mic (do not rely on camera audio alone for archival purposes).
