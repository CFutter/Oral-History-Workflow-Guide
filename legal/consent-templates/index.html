    Consent Form Templates

    All consent forms below are adapted from the UZH Declaration of Consent for Research Projects (version May 2018), supplemented with oral-history-specific provisions for each sensitivity tier. The language stays as close to the UZH original as possible, deviating only where oral history requires additional clauses.

    Two-stage consent: Use the Information Sheet (Stage 1) before the interview. Use the Consent Form (Stage 2) after the interview, when the narrator can make informed decisions about access, pseudonymization, and excluded passages based on what was actually discussed.

    Information Sheet (Stage 1 — All Tiers)

    This is provided to the narrator before the interview. It does not require a signature — it fulfills the duty to inform under Swiss data protection law.

    Information Sheet for Participants in an Oral History Research Project

    Title of research project:  

    Lead researcher:  

    Organizational unit at UZH:  

    Funded by:  

    Dear  ,

    You are invited to participate in an oral history research project conducted at the University of Zurich. This information sheet explains the purpose, methods, and your rights. Please read it carefully. You will have the opportunity to ask questions before and after the interview.

    What is the purpose of this project?

      (describe the research objectives in plain language)

    What will happen?

    You will be interviewed by   about your experiences, memories, and perspectives on  . The interview will be recorded in (audio / video / audio and video) format. The interview will last approximately   minutes. After the interview, a transcript will be produced.

    What personal data will be collected?

    The recording will capture your voice (and image, if video). During the interview, you may share personal experiences, opinions, and memories. Some of this may constitute sensitive personal data under Swiss data protection law (e.g., health experiences, political views, religious beliefs). You are free to decline to answer any question.

    How will your data be processed and stored?

    Your recording and transcript will be stored on secure University of Zurich infrastructure. Access is restricted to the research team:   (list team members). The data will be processed exclusively for academic research purposes.

    Who will have access?

    (Tier-specific: describe the access model — public / registered researchers / research team only / named individuals)

    How long will your data be kept?

    As a rule, the data is kept for a ten-year period and then deleted or destroyed, or has identifying information removed, unless permission has been given for its continued use in other research projects.

    Your rights

    You may at any time:

    • Request information about the data stored about you
    • Demand the correction, deletion, or sealing of your personal data
    • Object to the processing or transmission of your data
    • Withdraw your consent (see Consent Form for details)
    • Lodge a complaint with the competent supervisory authority

    Contact

    For questions about this project:   (lead researcher email)
    For data protection concerns: UZH Delegate for Data Protection, datenschutz@dsd.uzh.ch

    Consent Forms by Tier (Stage 2)

    The following consent forms are signed after the interview. Each builds on the UZH template with tier-specific additions.

    Tier 1 — Open

    Consent Form: Oral History Interview (Tier 1)

    Declaration of Consent for Research Projects

    (University of Zurich, adapted for oral history)

    By Ms / Mr:  

    Title of research project:  

    Organizational unit responsible at UZH:  

    Lead researcher:  

    I hereby consent that the following personal data may be recorded from me:

    Audio recording ☐   Video recording ☐   Written transcript ☐

    in connection with the oral history research project with the following objective:  .

    An individual within the responsible organizational unit at UZH,   (first name and last name), has comprehensively explained the purpose, importance and scope of gathering such data, as well as the precise way and location in which my data will be processed and the research project conducted. In this connection I had the opportunity to ask questions, understood the answers, and currently have no further questions about the research project. I had sufficient time to decide whether or not to participate in this research project.

    I am taking part voluntarily. I have been notified that I may revoke my consent at any time for the future, without giving reasons, and that all of the data that has been gathered up to that point will be deleted and destroyed, providing the deletion and destruction of the collected data does not make it impossible to achieve the objectives of the research or seriously jeopardizes their achievement. To revoke my consent, I will contact the aforementioned responsible organizational unit.

    I have been notified that I can apply at any time for comprehensive information about the data that is stored about me. I have also been notified that I can, at any time, demand the correction, deletion and sealing of individual items of personal data by the aforementioned responsible organizational unit, and can also object to the processing or transmission of such data.

    I am aware that the data about me will be used in this research project exclusively for academic purposes, and I consent to such use.

    Publication and identification

    My data may be used in a form that allows conclusions to be drawn about my identity (full name attribution).

    Access conditions

    I consent to the interview recording and transcript being made publicly accessible without restrictions.

    Once the research project has been concluded, the personal data will be handed over to   (name of archive) to be archived. As a rule, the data is kept for a ten-year period and then deleted or destroyed, or it has identifying information removed, unless permission has been given for its continued use in other research projects.

    I consent to be contacted in connection with future research projects.

    I have been informed that I may lodge any complaint with the competent supervisory authority.

    Place, date / signature

    Tiers 2 and 5

    Tier 2 uses the same structure as Tier 1 but adds: pseudonym option, excluded-passages clause, and conditions (e.g., no commercial use, attribution required). Tier 5 uses the Tier 4 structure but adds: named-individual access lists, potential destruction clause, and heightened re-consent requirements. Full templates for all five tiers will be available as downloadable .docx files.

    Additional Templates

    UZH Confidentiality Declaration

    Required for external persons (transcribers, research assistants, collaborators) who will have access to oral history data. Based on the UZH Confidentiality Declaration (Legal Services and Data Protection, January 2025).

    Key obligations for signatories:

    • Maintain confidentiality of all non-public information
    • Comply with the Zurich IDG
    • Process data only for the stipulated purposes
    • Return or destroy all data upon termination
    • Report any data loss or unauthorized access immediately

    → Obtain the template from UZH Legal Services / Delegate for Data Protection (datenschutz@dsd.uzh.ch)

    Data Sharing Agreement

    For sharing oral history data with external institutions, use the UZH Master Data Sharing Agreement (Controller–Controller). This covers data categories, purposes, security measures, breach notification, and data subject rights.

    → Obtain the template from UZH Legal Services
