## Audio Recording Equipment

All audio recorders and microphones listed here are local, offline hardware — recordings are stored on an SD card or connected device. **No data leaves your possession**, so all are suitable for every sensitivity tier.

| Equipment | Type | Approx. price | Tiers | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Zoom H6essential** | Portable recorder (6 inputs) | CHF 350–400 | 1–5 | Current model replacing H6. 4 XLR inputs, backup recording, 32-bit float. |
| **Zoom H1essential** | Compact portable recorder | CHF 100–120 | 1–5 | Budget-friendly, 32-bit float, good built-in mics. Simple one-on-one interviews. |
| **Tascam DR-40X** | Portable recorder (4 inputs) | CHF 200–250 | 1–5 | Reliable workhorse. Dual recording mode (backup at lower level). XLR inputs. |
| **Tascam DR-07X** | Compact portable recorder | CHF 100–130 | 1–5 | Good budget option. Adjustable stereo mics. USB audio interface mode. |
| **Rode NT1 5th Gen** | Large-diaphragm condenser mic | CHF 250–280 | 1–5 | Studio-quality for stationary setups. Very low noise. USB and XLR. |
| **Rode PodMic USB** | Dynamic USB/XLR mic | CHF 110–130 | 1–5 | Good for voice, rejects background noise. Plug-and-play USB option. |
| **Audio-Technica AT2020** | Condenser mic | CHF 100–120 | 1–5 | Affordable studio-quality. Good for quiet indoor settings. |
| **Rode Lavalier II** | Clip-on lavalier mic | CHF 100–120 | 1–5 | Discrete body mic. Good for video interviews where the mic shouldn't be visible. |
| **Rode Wireless GO II** | Wireless lavalier system (2 transmitters) | CHF 280–320 | 1–5 | Two wireless mics → excellent for interviewer + narrator. Built-in recording as backup. |

**Recommended minimum setup:** Zoom H1essential or Tascam DR-07X + a quiet room. For higher quality: Zoom H6essential + 2× Rode PodMic or lavalier mics.

**File formats:** Always record in WAV (uncompressed) at 48 kHz / 24-bit minimum. 32-bit float recorders (Zoom essential series) eliminate the risk of clipping.
